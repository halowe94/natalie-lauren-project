A Poem for Natalie
Engage Her Work
In your words
I find that there is space for me
To be
To breathe
To feel a multiplicity of feelings
And consider it beautiful
That in my feeling, deeply
I find connection
With my humanity
Inviting me to live
In the light of liberation
And the hope
Of the process.
In the space of your canvas
I am asked to remember
What maybe I never knew
Or maybe what surviving
Has tried to take from me
The memory
Our collective story
Shared within this skin
My tears friends
Speaking truth to me
When I would rather
Allow my smile to lie
Somehow I see myself reflected
In what you create.
Here still,
I find that it can all fit together
Oh to be Black and queer in love
And call it good
And believe that which flows from it
is goodness
To say that God is up to something good
And that good thing
Being me
Coming how to myself
Learning and relearning to love this skin
And to value my breath,
My body,
My art,
As sacred.
